Overview

A/B tests across the Tang Frères journey

This page shows all the experiments we conducted for the A/B testing.

Stage 1 · Awareness

How people discover Tang Frères Delivery and understand the value quickly.

Awareness · Homepage hero

Landing page: generic vs. “Plan your stir-fry in 3 clicks”

Compare a generic hero (Version A) with an intent-based hero including search and quick recipes (Version B).

A: “Asian groceries delivered to your door” + one CTA.

B: “Plan your stir-fry in 3 clicks” + search bar + recipe shortcuts.

Bounce rate −15% Hero CTA click-through +20%

Variant B focuses on a specific use case (tonight’s dinner), which typically improves clarity and engagement.

Awareness · Social ads

Social creative: static image vs. 15-second recipe video

We test how ad format influences traffic quality from paid social campaigns driving to the homepage.

A: Static image carousel featuring 3 Tang Frères products with price and logo.

B: 15-second recipe video showing the “Weeknight Stir-Fry” bundle being prepared.

Click-through rate +22% Landing-page sessions +18%

Variant B (video) usually drives more engaged traffic that stays longer and is more likely to explore recipes.

Stage 2 · Consideration

Help users explore the catalogue

These tests optimise how quickly shoppers discover relevant products and build a basket.

Search & filters

Filter prominence for pantry items

A: Basic product list, minimal filters.
B: Always-visible filters (noodles, frozen, sauces, vegan, spicy).

Product views per session +22% Add-to-cart rate +12%
Recipe bundle

Recipe page vs. one-click “Weeknight Stir-Fry” bundle

A: Users add ingredients one by one from the list.
B: One CTA adds the full stir-fry bundle to the cart.

Average basket size +18% Time to complete basket −25%
Stage 3 · Decision & purchase

Reduce friction at checkout

We compare a classic multi-step flow with mandatory login against a simpler guest-friendly flow.

Checkout flow

Four-step account flow vs. three-step simplified flow

A: Cart → Login → Address → Payment (account required).
B: Cart → Details → Payment (login optional).

Checkout completion rate +12% Average checkout time −10%
Guest checkout

Account required vs. “Continue as guest”

A: New customers must create an account before paying.
B: Guest checkout available, account creation offered after purchase.

New customer orders +19% Checkout abandonment −14%
Stage 4 · Delivery & use

Manage expectations after the order

Tests here focus on perceived reliability and how we handle out-of-stock items.

Delivery ETA

Broad 30–90 min window vs. 1-hour slot with precise ETA

A: Single message “delivery in 30–90 minutes”.
B: Customer chooses a 1-hour slot and sees a live ETA (e.g. 18:40–18:55).

On-time perception +18% Support tickets about delivery −12%
Substitution rules

Store-decided substitutions vs. customer-controlled rules

A: Store chooses substitutes, customer only sees refund.
B: Customer chooses: allow similar, SMS confirmation, or no substitutions.

Order satisfaction +22% Refund rate −9%
Stage 5 · Loyalty & advocacy

Encourage repeat orders and community content

Finally, we test alternative loyalty models and incentives for reviews and recipes.

Loyalty design

Flat points programme vs. Silver / Gold / VIP tiers

A: 1 point per €1, €5 coupon at 100 points, same for everyone.
B: Tiered benefits (Silver, Gold, VIP) with free delivery and early access.

Repeat orders per month +15% Average order value +6%
UGC incentives

Standard orders vs. extra points for reviews & recipes

A: No reward for reviews or photos.
B: Bonus points for sharing recipes, ratings and photos of meals.

UGC submissions +40% Organic traffic from SEO +9%
